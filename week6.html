Reconnaissance Research, identification, Selection of targets The hackers monitored and did research on the company, Solar winds, and its customers to determine which organizations would be valuable targets for their attack. One way to mitigate against reconnaissance is to implement strong perimeter defenses and to limit the information that is publicly available about your organization's assets and employees. This could include limiting the information that is posted on your organization's website, as well as implementing measures such as firewalls and intrusion detection systems to prevent unauthorized access.

This could include signing software updates with a digital certificate and verifying the signature before installation.

Delivery The means of attack is delivered in this stage The malicious update was delivered to SolarWinds customers and installed on their systems, through Orion software update. To mitigate against delivery, it is important to implement strong spam and email filtering to prevent malicious emails from reaching their intended targets. It is also important to keep all systems and software up to date with the latest security patches.

installation Installation of the malware or cyberweapon is carried out in this stage The attackers installed additional malware on the affected systems, including a tool called SUNBURST, which allowed them to maintain a presence on the system and move laterally within the victim's network. To mitigate against the installation of malware, it is important to implement strong access controls to prevent unauthorized users from gaining access to your systems. Command and control The attacker communicates with the malware The attackers established a command and control infrastructure to communicate with the SUNBURST malware and give it instructions. To mitigate against the establishment of a command and control infrastructure, it is important to implement strong perimeter defenses and to monitor your network for any unusual traffic patterns. It is also important to implement endpoint security measures to prevent malware from communicating with external servers.

Action on objective The main purpose of the attack is carried out The attackers used their access to the affected systems to gather information and exfiltrate data. It is not yet clear what their ultimate objectives were, but it is suspected that they were gathering intelligence for a nation-state. To mitigate against actions on objectives, it is important to implement strong access controls and to monitor your systems for any unusual activity. It is also important to have a plan in place for responding to a breach, including procedures for isolating affected systems and restoring them from known good backups.

My Reflection

 Overall, the SolarWinds exploit was a sophisticated and well-planned attack that took advantage of a supply chain vulnerability to gain access to a large number of organizations. The attackers demonstrated a high level of knowledge and expertise in the cyber domain, and the attack is still being investigated and understood by the cybersecurity community.
